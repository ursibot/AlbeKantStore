White Cube, 2021. Oil on canvas, 39 3/8 x 39 3/8 inches (100 x 100 cm)

$5000

Contrary to the latent implication of alienation and solitude represented by the frailly upraised white calla lily in the foreground of her cubic space, Albe Kant refrains from this reading of the work. The calla lily - a symbol of elegance, grace, pure reconciliation, and above all, autarkic self-sufficiency. In contrast to the prevalent comprehension, this artwork aims to illustrate the powerful emancipation one sustains through the act of an all-encompassing fusion or synthesis as the conscious and unconscious merge into unity. Inspired by Carl Gustav Jung’s writings on archetypes and the epistemic stage theory he proposed, wherewithin one attains a holistic vision of reality. This entails the union of all autonomies, upon which space and time, real and surreal, mundane and ethereal, feminine and masculine, fuse into one. In depicting an androgynous, hermaphrodite-like calla efflorescence, the artist exemplifies the stage of „Quintessenz“, professed as the last, just as the individual acquires supplementation within themselves. Albe Kant equates this estate with the White Cube - integral dispossession of reference within an aseptic environment.
